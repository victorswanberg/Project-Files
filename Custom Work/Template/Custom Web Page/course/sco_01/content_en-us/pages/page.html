Track and test your results.

Programmatic gives you the flexibility to make spontaneous adjustments. For example, if an ad isn’t performing well, you can shut it off and refocus your budget elsewhere. But don’t be too hasty—wait until you’ve gathered sufficient data that supports your decision to switch tactics.

Use multi-touch contribution models.

These go beyond single-touch attribution models, which only tell you the action that immediately preceded a sale. Multi-touch models reveal the combination of actions that led to a purchase. They offer more depth and a more detailed picture of the customer journey.

Focus on creative quality.

It’s not just about reaching the right people. It’s also about what happens once you reach them. You need a compelling story, captivating visuals, and a strong call to action to bring results. If you’re reaching the right customers but not generating enough conversions, try adjusting your creative.

Collaborate across channels.

Communicate with colleagues working in the email and social media channels. By sharing your insights, you’ll get a better sense of the kinds of audience segments you need and how to reach them.

Start thinking about how you can use these tips to maximize the impact of your programmatic marketing initiatives.
